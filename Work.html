My Story as a Software developer

2017

In 2017, I participated in my first science fair. Using Arduino, I created a better smart lighting system by using ambient light sensors and motion sensors. I won the Young Innovator Award in my highschool Royal West Academy.

2018

In my Ed.Tech classroom, whenever we finished our homework we would have to do online typing tests and the top student would have bragging rights. So I created a Selenium Python code which will allow me to achieve speeds that no human has ever attained.

2019

In 2019, I participated in the science fair with the same idea but instead of Arduino, I used microcontrollers and PCBs which made it more practical and realistic. The new smart lighting system saved 80% more electricity than common lighting systems.

2019-2020

Paramount Study hired me to teach students programmming for their old bootcamp which was focused on teaching Python but the students were easily bored and I suggested in incorporating an Arduino Bootcamp where students were able to see what they coded rather than a boring terminal. Over 50 students attended this Bootcamp.

2020

During this pandemic, I was introduced to the stock market. Especially during the pandemic, the overall stock market was very volatile. In order to take full advantage of this, I coded an AI Day-Trading Bot which was able to secure 1% of profit daily by using several concepts of Calculus in order to predict the rise or fall of the market.

Summer 2020

During Summer 2020, there was a startup company for Web development and I was the only intern in the startup and I was able to participate in all the stages in making a website, such as the Front-End,Back-End development of a website with the use of Javascript, HTML and CSS.

Spring 2020

DataCare was my first hackathon project where we tackle important heathcare system issues. My team and I worked on a new, simple platform for nursing homes that will allow to reduce physical interaction during COVID-19. We were able to create this working prototype in 20 hours!
